# Lab-3-ML

In this assignment, an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) was performed on the Student Performance dataset to understand the structure, patterns, and relationships within the data before applying any advanced analysis.

First, the dataset structure was examined by reviewing the number of rows and columns, data types, and checking for any missing values. The dataset was relatively clean, allowing for direct analysis without extensive preprocessing.

Next, descriptive statistics were calculated to summarize the main characteristics of the numerical variables, including math, reading, and writing scores. Measures such as mean, minimum, maximum, and distribution spread helped provide an overview of student performance across subjects.

Data visualization techniques were then applied to explore patterns and relationships:
• Histograms were used to analyze the distribution of scores in each subject.  
• Boxplots were created to observe score spread and detect potential outliers.  
• Bar charts were used to compare performance across categories such as gender and test preparation course.  
• Scatter plots were used to examine relationships between subjects.  
• Correlation analysis was performed to measure the strength of relationships between scores.

The analysis revealed several key findings:
• There is a strong positive correlation between all subjects.  
• Students who perform well in one subject tend to perform well in others.  
• Reading and writing scores are generally higher than math scores.  
• Females tend to perform better in reading and writing, while males slightly lead in math.  
• Completing the test preparation course is associated with improved performance.

Overall, the EDA provided a clear understanding of score distributions, performance patterns, and relationships between subjects, offering useful insights into student academic performance.
